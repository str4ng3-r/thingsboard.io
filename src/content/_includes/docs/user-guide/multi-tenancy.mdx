<ConditionalHeading level={2} id="best-practices" showFor="paas,paas-eu">Best Practices</ConditionalHeading>

To keep your Cloud tenant secure and running smoothly:
- Assign at least one additional <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/users' target="_blank">User</DocLink> to the built-in **Tenant Administrators** group (see <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/roles#tenant-defaults' target="_blank">Default User Groups and Roles</DocLink>) for redundancy.
- Monitor your entity and API usage against your <DocLink product={props.product} path='reference/subscriptions' target="_blank">subscription plan's limits</DocLink> to avoid disruptions as you scale.
- Apply consistent <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/white-labeling' target="_blank">White-labeling</DocLink> and a custom <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/security/domains' target="_blank">domain</DocLink> to keep your tenant on-brand for customers.
- Enable <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/security/two-factor-authentication' target="_blank">Two-Factor Authentication</DocLink> and review the <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/security/audit-log' target="_blank">Audit Log</DocLink> regularly to keep tenant access secure.
- Use consistent naming conventions for your Customers and User Groups.

See <DocLink product={props.product} path='concepts/multi-tenancy' target="_blank">Multi-Tenancy & Hierarchy</DocLink> for an overview of the relationships between **System Administrator**, **Tenant**, **Customer**, and **User**.

<ConditionalHeading level={2} id="tenants-list" showFor="ce,pe">Tenants List</ConditionalHeading>

**System Administrators** create and manage Tenants from the **Tenants** page in the sidebar. The list shows each tenant's title, tenant profile, and creation date, and lets you search by name — from here you can open a tenant to view or edit its details, manage its administrators, or create a new tenant.

<ConditionalHeading level={2} id="tenant-management" showFor="ce,pe">Tenant Management</ConditionalHeading>

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="create-tenant" showFor="ce,pe">Create Tenant</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
1. Log in to ThingsBoard as a **System Administrator**.
2. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
3. Click the **+ Add tenant** in the top right corner.
4. Enter a **title** (the tenant's display name).
5. Use the default <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/tenant-profiles' target="_blank">tenant profile</DocLink> or select a different one.
6. Optionally, provide an address, phone number, contact email.
7. Click **Add**.
</Steps>

Once created, the tenant becomes available in the tenants list.

---

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="edit-tenant" showFor="ce,pe">Edit Tenant</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
2. Click the tenant row to open its details.
3. Click the orange **pencil** icon (**Edit**) to enter edit mode.
4. Update the tenant information.
5. **Apply changes**.
</Steps>

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="delete-tenant" showFor="ce,pe">Delete Tenant</ConditionalHeading>

<Aside type="note">
There's no separate action to suspend or temporarily deactivate a tenant. To block access without losing data, disable its administrators (see <a href="#enable-disable-tenant-administrator">Enable/Disable Tenant Administrator</a>) below; to remove the tenant entirely, delete it.
</Aside>

<Steps>
1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
2. Click the **trash** icon next to the tenant, or open the tenant details page and click **Delete tenant**.
3. Confirm the deletion.
</Steps>

<Aside type="danger" title="Warning">
  Deleting a tenant removes all tenant-related data and entities.
</Aside>

<ConditionalHeading level={2} id="tenant-administrator-management" showFor="ce,pe">Tenant Administrator Management</ConditionalHeading>

A **Tenant Administrator** manages all tenant-scoped entities and configuration for a single tenant — see <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/roles' target="_blank">Roles</DocLink> for the full permission model and <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/users' target="_blank">Users</DocLink> for general user management within a tenant. All operations are isolated within the tenant context and do not affect other tenants in the system.

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="create-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Create Tenant Administrator</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
2. In the **Tenants** list, click **Manage tenant admins** for the tenant.
3. Click **+ Add user** in the top right corner.
4. Enter the user's **email address**.
  Optionally, provide first and last name, and phone number.
5. Choose the user activation method:
    - **Display activation link** — generates an activation link that you can copy and share manually.
    - **Send activation mail** — sends the activation link to the specified email address (requires a configured <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/ui/mail-settings' target="_blank">mail server</DocLink>).
6. Click **Add**.
</Steps>

After creation, the Tenant Administrator can log in and manage tenant resources.

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="edit-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Edit Tenant Administrator</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
  1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
  2. Click **Manage tenant admins** for the tenant.
  3. Click the Tenant Administrator row to open its details.
  4. Click the orange **pencil** icon (**Edit**) to enter edit mode.
  5. Update the tenant information.
  6. Apply changes.
</Steps>

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="enable-disable-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Enable/Disable Tenant Administrator</ConditionalHeading>

ThingsBoard allows administrators to temporarily block a user's access to the platform without deleting the account.
Disabled users cannot log in or access any tenant resources.

To enable or disable a user account:

<Steps>
1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
2. Click **Manage tenant admins** for the desired tenant.
3. Click the User row to open its details.
4. Click **Enable/Disable User Account**.
</Steps>

Disabling a Tenant Administrator immediately signs them out and blocks further login, but doesn't affect the tenant's data — resources remain fully accessible to any other Tenant Administrator on the same tenant.

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="login-as-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Login as Tenant Administrator</ConditionalHeading>

System Administrators can log in as a Tenant Administrator directly from the tenants list by using the **Login as tenant admin** action.

This is useful for troubleshooting tenant-specific issues and verifying tenant configuration.

<ConditionalHeading level={3} id="delete-tenant-administrator" showFor="ce,pe">Delete Tenant Administrator</ConditionalHeading>

<Steps>
1. From the sidebar, navigate to **Tenants**.
2. Click **Manage tenant admins** for the tenant.
3. Click the **trash** icon next to the Tenant Administrator user, or open the user details page and click **Delete user**.
4. Confirm the deletion.
</Steps>

<Aside type="danger" title="Warning">
  Deleting a Tenant Administrator **permanently deletes** the user account and all related data.

  Tenant data — including entities, telemetry, etc. — is not affected and remains intact.
</Aside>

<ConditionalHeading level={2} id="best-practices" showFor="ce,pe">Best Practices</ConditionalHeading>

To ensure stable and secure multi-tenant deployments:
- Use <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/tenant-profiles' target="_blank">Tenant Profiles</DocLink> to enforce quotas and prevent excessive resource usage.
- Assign at least two Tenant Administrators per tenant for redundancy.
- Use consistent naming conventions for tenants and customer structures.
- Monitor message throughput and entity limits for each tenant.
- Apply consistent <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/white-labeling' target="_blank">White-labeling</DocLink> per tenant to keep each deployment on-brand.
- Review <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/security' target="_blank">Security</DocLink> settings — password policy and JWT session limits apply across all tenants.

For enterprise environments with many users sharing devices and dashboards, replace the fixed roles with <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/roles' target="_blank">custom roles</DocLink> and <DocLink product={props.product} path='user-guide/groups' target="_blank">entity groups</DocLink> to scope access to specific subsets of a tenant's resources instead of the whole tenant.
